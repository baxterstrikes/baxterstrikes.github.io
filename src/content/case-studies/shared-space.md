> **Placeholder — concept in progress.** This slot exists so the work has a home before it's built. The seed is real; the case study is not written yet.

### The seed

A voice memo I sent myself, April 2026:

*"Picture this — a kid hotel. All the trappings of a daycare, but instead of 'do you need care? do we have a contract?' … open every day, drop your kid off for a day, or don't book ahead. Does that business model work?"*

Drop-in childcare with no enrollment, no contract, book-by-day or walk-in — and, in the version worth designing, a place the parent can actually *work* while they're there.

### What this case study has to answer

- **The job.** What is the real recurring moment this serves — and for whom, exactly? (Not "parents." Which parents, at what intensity of pain?)
- **The model.** Does drop-in-without-contract actually pencil out, or does the math force you back toward enrollment?
- **The space.** What does a room have to be to hold both cared-for kids and working adults without either ruining the other?
- **The design artifact.** This is a net-new concept, so the deliverable is the *design of the investigation* — not a launched business.
